What it is

One workflow for the whole clip pipeline

Clipping a livestream by hand means watching for hours, cutting, reframing to vertical, adding a title and captions, exporting, and uploading to each platform one at a time. AstroClipz collapses that into a single automated pipeline with a human approving every clip before it goes out.

Catches the moment

It watches live streams it's authorised to clip and flags high-energy moments as they happen — no scrubbing back through a multi-hour VOD.

Formats for vertical

Every clip is reframed to a 9:16 vertical canvas with a headline, captions and branding — ready for TikTok, Shorts and Reels without opening a video editor.

Keeps a human in the loop

Nothing posts on its own. Each clip lands in a review queue to be trimmed, edited and approved — then published through the platforms' official APIs.

Features

Everything a clip needs, in one place

The pieces that usually mean five different apps — a recorder, an editor, a captioner, a brander and an uploader — built into one tool.

Automatic moment detection

A rolling buffer records the stream while live chat activity is analysed in real time, so spikes of hype are captured the instant they happen.

Vertical formatting

Gameplay is scaled into a 9:16 frame over a clean black or blurred-fill background, with an editable headline placed exactly where you want it.

Word-by-word captions

Speech is transcribed automatically into animated captions that you can correct and reposition — the retention-boosting subtitles short-form needs.

Branding & watermarks

Add a platform lockup and handle, or drop in a custom campaign watermark — sized, placed and opacity-tuned per clip, or turned off entirely.

Review & one-tap publish

Approve a clip and publish straight to a connected account through the official Content Posting API, with a caption-and-hashtag editor built in.

Made for a posting cadence

A mobile-friendly review app lets you clear the queue from anywhere, so consistent, well-timed posting stays realistic day after day.

How it works

Five steps, mostly automatic

You point it at a stream and clear the review queue. Everything in between runs on its own.

01

Capture

A continuous rolling buffer records the live stream so no moment is missed, keeping only a recent window at any time.

02

Detect

Chat velocity and engagement are measured live; when a moment spikes past a per-streamer threshold, that window is clipped out automatically.

03

Edit & format

The clip is cut to a vertical 9:16 frame with a headline, auto-captions and branding — the packaging that makes a clip post-ready.

04

Review & approve

The finished clip waits in a private queue. You trim, tweak the caption and hashtags, and approve — or reject it. Nothing posts without you.

05

Publish

Approved clips upload to connected accounts through each platform's official API, formatted correctly for TikTok, Shorts and Reels.
